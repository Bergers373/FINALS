Louvre Museum



          One of the many places I’d love to visit is the Louvre Museum in Paris, France. It is known as the world’s largest museum where the most impressive art collections in history are placed. The museum is magnificent because of its baroque-style palace-like structure when it was built and renovated. Jessie Szalay (2018) stated that the Louvre was originally built as a fortress during the 1190’s but was reconstructed to serve as a royal palace during the 16th century. When it was still a royal residence, the tremendous growth of the said palace came into view because of every monarch who expanded it. When Louis XIV moved the royal residence to Versailles in 1682, the Louvre became a place to various art academies.


How to go there?


          There are many travel agencies that offers a one-way flight or a two-way flight from Philippines to France, or vice versa. Clients are provided with packages that give tourists the choice that best suits their preference, so that they are good to go. However, if the person wishes to travel alone, in order to get to the Louvre Museum, book a plane ticket going to France. The travel time of air transport from the Philippines to France takes for about almost 19 hours. Once the destination is reached, book a cab or any road transport that can send you to the Louvre Museum in Rue de Rivoli which will take for about 40 minutes.
