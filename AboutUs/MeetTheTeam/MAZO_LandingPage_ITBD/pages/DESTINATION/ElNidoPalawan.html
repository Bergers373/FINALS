El Nido Palawan



          There is also a place in my home country that I’d love to visit and that is El Nido, Palawan. It is in the northern part of the Palawan province that is blessed with pristine beaches, crystal-clear water, and a wealth of flaura and fauna. Talindak was the first recorded name of El Nido which later then was renamed as Bacuit by the Spaniards. After discovering the nests of swiftlets in the caves of its 250 million year-old limestone cliffs, the name Bacuit was changes into “El Nido” in 1954 which means “The Nest” in Spanish (Kasaysayan, 2011).


How to go there?


          A flight going from Metro Manila to Puerto Princesa Airport usually takes for an hour and a half. Once arrived, the person can use ride a bus or a van for 5-7 hours going to El Nido. Vans are more recommended than any other road transportation because it is slightly faster than the bus travel which takes for about 5-6 hours before reaching the destination.


Other places to visit in this country


          Because of the rich natural resources of Palawan, one of its tourist attractions is the Tabon Caves in the southern part of Palawan. The said archeological site is where the Tabon man was found. It is believed that the man’s fossilized remains is said to be the earliest human remains known in the Philippines. Moreover, the cave is also famous for its impressive limestone formations and the crystal-clear water. Traveling in this sport is best for archeology, history lovers, and adventure seekers.
