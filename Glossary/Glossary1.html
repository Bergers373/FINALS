Glossary


Fulfill

Bring to completion or reality; achieve or realize (something desired, promised, or predicted).

Implement

A tool, utensil, or other piece of equipment, especially as used for a particular purpose.

Insights

The capacity to gain an accurate and deep intuitive understanding of a person or thing.

Knowledgeable

Intelligent and well informed.

Management

The process of dealing with or controlling things or people.

Mediums

An agency or means of doing something.

Organize

Arrange into a structured whole; order.

Passion

Strong liking or desire

Practitioner

A person actively engaged in an art, discipline, or profession, especially medicine.

Precisely

In exact terms; without vagueness.

Reliable

Consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted.

Role

The function assumed or part played by a person or thing in a particular situation.

Seek

Attempt to find (something).

Sustain

Strengthen or support physically or mentally.

Self-disciplined

The ability to control themselves and to make themselves work hard or behave in a particular way without needing anyone else to tell them what to do.

Software development

Refers to a set of computer science activities dedicated to the process of creating, designing, deploying and supporting software

Specialization

The process of concentrating on and becoming expert in a particular subject or skill.

Specific

Relating to or connected with species or a species.

Standalone software

Unavailability

The state of being otherwise occupied or not being free to do something.

Variety

The characteristic of frequently changing, or of including many different types or things
